Facebook Business Page Basics

Get established on Facebook — a free way to give customers a familiar social place to find, follow, and message your business.

A Facebook business Page is a public professional profile for a business, brand, organization, or project. This page walks you through creating or setting one up: what to do, what to gather first, and the official Meta links — without promising followers, leads, or ad results. Treat it as one practical external profile, not a full social media plan.

Every official Meta link you need to get established with a Facebook Page is on this page — follow the checklist below, one step at a time.

Basic Starting Path

You do not need a formal business plan, but at least a short sketch of what you do, who you serve, and how customers reach you will make the steps below much easier.

  1. Gather at least the minimal information you will need — Page name, category, bio, contact details, location or service area, hours, website, and images. Use Basic Information To Gather below as your checklist.
  2. Decide which Facebook profile should create or own the Page. Use an account the business owner controls, and document backup access decisions.
  3. Create a Facebook Page for the business or use the Facebook Pages creation flow.
  4. Add the Page name and best-fit category.
  5. Add a short bio, phone number, website, email address, location or service area, hours, and other core details.
  6. Add a recognizable profile picture and cover photo.
  7. Set the action button if one fits the business, such as call, message, book, or visit website.
  8. Review Page notifications and messaging settings so customers are not missed.
  9. If more than one person will help manage the Page, use Meta's Page access or business portfolio tools rather than sharing passwords.
  10. Keep the Page current when hours, services, location, ownership, or contact details change.

Basic Information To Gather

  • Business or organization name as it should appear publicly.
  • Best-fit Facebook Page category.
  • One- or two-sentence bio.
  • Phone number.
  • Website URL.
  • Email address or contact path.
  • Address, service area, or note that the business is online-only.
  • Hours.
  • Services, products, menu, appointment link, or booking link if relevant.
  • Profile picture, logo, and cover image.
  • Facebook profile owner and backup manager decisions.
  • Meta Business Suite or business portfolio decision if multiple people or assets will be managed.
  • Messaging owner: the person responsible for responding to Page messages.

How Facebook Page Listing Works

A Facebook Page is different from a personal Facebook profile. The Page is the public business presence; the profile is the account a person uses to create or manage that Page. Customers can follow the Page, message it, see updates, and use the contact details you publish.

For many small businesses, a Facebook Page is useful because customers may search Facebook directly, click through from a shared post, or send a message instead of calling. It is also a common place to publish basic updates, photos, events, and service information.

If more than one person will help, use Meta's access tools. Do not share one personal login across a team. Keep at least one trusted owner documented so the business does not lose control of the Page.

Copy For A Website Section

A Facebook business Page can be part of a basic online-presence setup for a local business, service provider, creator, or organization. Start by creating a Page from an owner-controlled Facebook profile, then add the category, bio, contact information, hours, website, profile image, and cover image.

After setup, keep the Page current and decide who responds to messages. If several people help manage the business, use Page access or Meta Business Suite instead of sharing passwords.
