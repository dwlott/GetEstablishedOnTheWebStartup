Better Business Bureau Profile Basics

Get established on BBB — a free business profile path, with paid accreditation as a separate optional step.

A BBB Business Profile is a listing on BBB.org where customers can find basic business information and BBB-related trust signals. This page walks you through claiming or requesting a free profile: what to do, what to gather first, and the official BBB links — without promising rating, accreditation, leads, or approval. Treat it as one practical external profile, not a full reputation-management plan.

Every official BBB link you need to get established with a BBB profile is on this page — follow the checklist below, one step at a time.

Basic Starting Path

You do not need a formal business plan, but at least a short sketch of what you do, who you serve, and how customers reach you will make the steps below much easier.

  1. Gather at least the minimal information you will need — legal business name, public business name, address, phone number, website, category, and ownership contact. Use Basic Information To Gather below as your checklist.
  2. Open BBB's get-listed page.
  3. Search for the business first to see whether BBB already has a Business Profile.
  4. If a Business Profile already exists, follow BBB's claim flow for the correct business.
  5. If no profile exists, submit a request to add a new BBB Business Profile.
  6. Use an owner or authorized representative contact who can respond if BBB asks for verification or clarification.
  7. Confirm the public profile details: business name, address or service area, phone, website, category, and description.
  8. Decide separately whether BBB Accreditation is worth exploring. Accreditation is not the same thing as a free profile.
  9. If pursuing accreditation, review BBB's accreditation standards and local BBB requirements before applying.
  10. Keep the profile current when the business moves, changes ownership, changes contact details, or closes.

Basic Information To Gather

  • Legal business name.
  • Public business name or DBA.
  • Business category.
  • Address, service area, or mailing address.
  • Phone number.
  • Website URL.
  • Email address for the owner or authorized representative.
  • Hours or appointment availability.
  • Business description.
  • Services or products.
  • Business start date.
  • Licensing or registration details if relevant.
  • Local BBB office or region if BBB routes the profile locally.
  • Decision note: free profile only, learn about accreditation, or apply later.

How BBB Profile Listing Works

BBB offers more than one business path. A free business listing/profile is the basic presence on BBB.org. BBB Accreditation is separate: it involves standards, review, eligibility, and annual dues when approved.

Start with the free listing path unless the business is intentionally ready to evaluate accreditation. BBB's own guidance separates "Get free listing," "Learn about BBB Accreditation," and "Apply for BBB Accreditation." Keeping those paths separate helps avoid confusing a simple profile setup with a paid trust program.

If there is already a BBB profile for the business, claim or update the correct profile instead of creating a duplicate. If the profile is controlled by someone else or routed to a local BBB office, use BBB's official support and local process.

Copy For A Website Section

A BBB Business Profile can be part of a basic online-presence setup for a local or service business. Start by searching BBB's get-listed page for the business, claim an existing profile if one appears, or submit a request to add a new free profile if needed.

After setup, keep the business name, category, address or service area, phone, website, and description accurate. Treat BBB Accreditation as a separate owner decision because it has standards, review, and annual dues.
