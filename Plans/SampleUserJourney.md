# Sample User Journey

A concrete example of a calm first session, from goals to reviewed drafts. Use it
to picture the flow — your own path will differ.

## Session In Motion

1. **Trigger Quick Startup.** The owner says
   *"Begin Quick Startup from AGENTS.md."* The agent reads `AGENTS.md` and
   `Capabilities/GettingStarted/GettingStarted.md`.
2. **Name Owner Goals.** The owner writes three practical outcomes in
   `Workspace/OwnerGoals.md`, for example:
   - get a basic website online;
   - improve a Google Business Profile;
   - draft two service pages.
3. **Check for prior material.** The agent asks whether there is a prior
   repository or archive to import from. If not, it continues.
4. **Capture candidate references.** The agent adds two to five `Suggested`
   rows to `Workspace/ValuableReferences.md` with a short note on each.
5. **Route one next task.** Per
   `Capabilities/GettingStarted/PostQuickStartupRouting.md`, the agent picks a
   single next step — here, drafting a service page with **OnePageWebsite** or
   reviewing example pages with **ContentReview**.
6. **Capture an idea.** A stray idea ("add a request-an-estimate button") is
   parked in `Ideas/FutureIdeas.md`, then promoted to `Plans/Ideas.md` when it
   has enough shape.
7. **Shape a small Plan.** Before building, the agent writes a short plan noting
   what happens now and what waits (no public launch without owner approval).
8. **Draft and review.** A page draft lands under `Content/`, and the owner
   reviews it before treating it as final.

## What Good Early Progress Looks Like

Early progress does **not** require a finished website. It looks like: goals
recorded, a few references captured, one small task chosen, a short plan written,
and a draft reviewed.
