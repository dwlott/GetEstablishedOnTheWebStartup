# Goals, Ideas, Plans, Capabilities Model

This is the plain-language flow the repository uses to carry work from intention
to outcome, in small reviewable steps.

```text
Goals decide what matters (Owner Goals from Quick Startup; Repository Goals for the core set).
Ideas are captured before they disappear.
Selected Ideas are nurtured into Plans.
Plans mature through review, sequencing, and small work passes.
Repeatable Plans can become Capabilities.
Capabilities help future humans and agents do better work without starting over.
```

## The Four Stages

- **Goals** — What matters. Two kinds:
  - **Owner Goals** ([../Workspace/OwnerGoals.md](../Workspace/OwnerGoals.md)) —
    the practical outcomes a specific owner wants, captured in Quick Startup.
  - **Repository Goals** ([RepositoryGoals.md](RepositoryGoals.md)) — the core,
    durable goals for how the repository stays organized and safe.
- **Ideas** — Rough by design. An Idea only needs enough shape to save. Park raw
  ideas in `Ideas/`, then promote worthwhile ones to the curated register in
  [Ideas.md](Ideas.md).
- **Plans** — More structured. A Plan explains what should happen, what should
  wait, and what boundaries protect the work.
- **Capabilities** — For repeatable work. When the same kind of task keeps coming
  back, a Capability collects the rules, prompts, and setup notes so future
  humans and agents can run it again. See `Capabilities/README.md`.

## Why This Order

Deciding Goals first prevents rushing into tools. Capturing Ideas prevents loss.
Shaping Plans prevents half-considered execution. Promoting Capabilities prevents
re-deriving the same work. The result is a workspace that is indexed, modular,
customizable, and ready to expand only when there is a real need.
