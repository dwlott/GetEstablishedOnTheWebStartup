# Nextdoor Business Page Basics

**Get established on Nextdoor** — a free local business profile for connecting with nearby customers and neighbors.

A Nextdoor Business Page is a free profile for a local business, service provider, or organization. This page walks you through claiming or creating one: what to do, what to gather first, and the [official Nextdoor links](#official-nextdoor-links) — without promising recommendations, posts, ads, or leads. Treat it as one practical external profile, not a full neighborhood marketing plan.

Every **official Nextdoor link** you need to get established on Nextdoor is on this page — follow the checklist below, one step at a time.

## Basic Starting Path

You do not need a formal business plan, but at least a short sketch of what you
do, who you serve, and how customers reach you will make the steps below much
easier.

1. Gather at least the minimal information you will need — business name,
   category, location or service area, phone number, website, and business
   description. Use **[Basic Information To Gather](#basic-information-to-gather)**
   below as your checklist.
2. [Open the Nextdoor Business Page setup page](https://business.nextdoor.com/en-us/getting-started/business-page).
3. Choose the free Business Page path and sign in or create an account.
4. Search for the business by name and location before creating anything new.
5. If a Business Page already exists, claim it as an owner, employee, or other
   authorized representative.
6. If the business does not appear, create a new Business Page.
7. Confirm name, category, contact information, service area or address display,
   website, and description.
8. Add useful images and business details that help neighbors recognize what you
   do.
9. Complete any verification or documentation steps Nextdoor asks for before
   posting or using business features.
10. Keep the Page current when hours, services, location, ownership, or contact
    details change.

## Basic Information To Gather

- Business name as it is used locally.
- Business category.
- Street address, service area, or note that the address should stay hidden.
- Phone number.
- Website URL.
- Hours or appointment availability.
- Business description.
- Services or products.
- Useful business photos or logo.
- Nextdoor account owner and backup manager decisions.
- Proof or documentation if Nextdoor asks to verify the business.
- Messaging owner: the person responsible for checking neighbor messages.

## How Nextdoor Business Page Listing Works

Nextdoor is neighborhood-focused. A Business Page is most useful when the
business serves a local area: home services, movers, contractors, personal care,
shops, restaurants, pet services, wellness, professional services, and similar
local work.

Nextdoor may already have business information from public data or neighbor
activity, so search first and claim the correct Page when it exists. If you do
not see the business, create a new Page through the official business setup
flow.

Some Nextdoor business features may require verification or business documents.
Use the prompts shown in your account and avoid creating duplicate Pages for the
same business.

## Copy For A Website Section

A Nextdoor Business Page can be part of a basic online-presence setup for a
local business. Start by searching for the business in Nextdoor's business setup
flow, claim the existing Page if one appears, or create a new Business Page if
needed.

After setup, keep the business name, category, contact information, website,
service area, photos, and description accurate. Assign one person to watch
messages and updates so neighbor inquiries do not get missed.
