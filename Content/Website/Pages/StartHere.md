# Start Here

You do not need a website platform on day one. You need sensible first steps.

## Choose Your Path

### Path A — Practical basics (most visitors)

Start with what people actually search for:

1. **[Google Business Profile Basics](GoogleBusinessProfileBasics.md)** — claim or add your business on Google Search and Maps.
2. **[Yelp Basics](YelpBasics.md)** — claim or add your business on Yelp.
3. **[Apple Maps Basics](AppleMapsBasics.md)** — claim or add your location on Apple Maps through Apple Business.
4. **[Bing Places Basics](BingPlacesBasics.md)** — claim or create your business listing on Bing Search and Maps.
5. **[Yellow Pages Basics](YellowPagesBasics.md)** — claim or add your business on YP.com (The Real Yellow Pages).

These pages are checklists and official links — not a full marketing plan. They
help you move forward without overthinking tools.

### Path B — GetEstablishedOnTheWeb repository

**GetEstablishedOnTheWebStartup** is the main starter package for this site:
the GetEstablished core plus web Capabilities for Google, Yelp, site content,
and growing modules for WordPress and related workflows.

When the download is published, you will:

1. Extract the package to a stable folder (not `Downloads`).
2. Open it in your editor and read `AGENTS.md`.
3. Run **Quick Startup** to write Owner Goals.
4. Connect GitHub when ready — see `GitHubWorkflow.md`.

If you do not want the web package, **GetEstablishedStartup** is the lighter
core-only repository. See `Offers.md` for how the packages relate.

## After the Basics

Once Google or Yelp is underway (or if you skipped straight to the repository):

1. Clarify who you are, who you serve, and what visitors should understand first.
2. Draft first website pages in plain Markdown before choosing WordPress or other platforms.
3. Use AI assistants on focused, reviewable tasks from your repository files.
4. Track open questions instead of blocking progress.

## What To Create First

- A short identity statement.
- A target audience note.
- A draft offer direction.
- A simple page list.
- Proof or credibility notes you can support honestly.
