# Foursquare Business Listing Basics

**Get established on Foursquare** — a location-data listing that can help your business details stay accurate across Foursquare's ecosystem.

A Foursquare business listing is a venue record that can show your business name, location, contact details, hours, website, and related place information. This page walks you through finding, claiming, or creating one: what to do, what to gather first, and the [official Foursquare links](#official-foursquare-links) — without promising traffic, downstream placement, or search results. Treat it as one practical external profile, not a full listings-data strategy.

Every **official Foursquare link** you need to get established on Foursquare is on this page — follow the checklist below, one step at a time.

## Basic Starting Path

You do not need a formal business plan, but at least a short sketch of what you
do, who you serve, and how customers reach you will make the steps below much
easier.

1. Gather at least the minimal information you will need — business name,
   category, address, phone number, hours, website, and description. Use
   **[Basic Information To Gather](#basic-information-to-gather)** below as your
   checklist.
2. [Open Foursquare Business Listings](https://business.foursquare.com/).
3. Search for the business by name and location before creating anything new.
4. If the business already appears, confirm it is the correct venue and start the
   claim process.
5. If the business does not appear, use Foursquare's add or setup path to create
   a new venue listing.
6. Complete the claim form with accurate contact details and your role with the
   business.
7. Complete any verification or review steps Foursquare requires.
8. Confirm the listing details: business name, address, phone, website, hours,
   categories, and description.
9. If the listing is already claimed by someone else, use Foursquare Support
   rather than creating a duplicate venue.
10. Keep the listing current when the business moves, closes, changes hours, or
    changes ownership.

## Basic Information To Gather

- Business name as it is used in the real world.
- Business category.
- Street address and map location.
- Phone number.
- Website URL.
- Hours.
- Business description.
- Services or products.
- Useful business photos if the setup flow asks for them.
- Foursquare account owner and backup manager decisions.
- Claim contact: the person who can confirm their role with the business.
- Notes on any existing Foursquare venue URLs that may be duplicates.

## How Foursquare Listing Works

Foursquare is a location-data and discovery platform. A business may already
exist as a venue because customers, public data, or data partners created a
record. Search first, claim the correct listing when it exists, and avoid
creating duplicate venues for the same location.

Keeping Foursquare data accurate can matter beyond the Foursquare app because
Foursquare promotes its business listing ecosystem as connected to partner
platforms and location-data uses. That does not guarantee where your business
will appear, but it does make the listing worth checking if you want consistent
business details across the web.

If the listing is already claimed by another owner or manager, follow
Foursquare's support process. Do not create a second listing to work around an
ownership problem.

## Copy For A Website Section

Foursquare can be part of a basic online-presence setup for a location-based
business. Start by searching Foursquare Business Listings for the business name
and location. Claim the correct venue if it already exists, or create a new one
if needed.

After setup, keep the name, category, address, phone number, website, hours, and
description accurate. Watch for duplicate venues and use Foursquare Support if
the correct listing is already claimed by someone else.
